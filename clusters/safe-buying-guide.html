Safe Sizing Guide: How to Choose the Right Size Safe

Choosing the right safe size is one of the most important buying decisions. This guide helps customers plan for storage, space, growth, delivery, and installation.

Why Safe Size Matters

One of the most common mistakes buyers make is choosing a safe that is too small. A safe should fit what you own today and leave room for future growth.

Plan What You’ll Store

Think about documents, jewelry, valuables, firearms, cash, digital media, and sentimental items. Choose a safe 20–30% larger than your current needs.

Compare Interior Capacity

Exterior dimensions show whether a safe fits your space, but interior dimensions determine what actually fits inside.

Measure the Full Path

Measure doorways, hallways, staircases, corners, flooring, and final placement space before selecting a safe.

Plan Delivery Early

A safe may fit the room but still be difficult to maneuver. Delivery and installation planning should happen before purchase.

Safe Size Categories

Small Safes

Best for documents, cash, jewelry, handguns, and compact valuables.

Medium Safes

Best for binders, multiple handguns, electronics, valuables, and mixed storage.

Large Safes

Best for rifle collections, shotgun storage, bulk storage, and long-term growth.

Gun Capacity Reality

Manufacturer gun counts are optimistic. Scoped rifles, accessories, and shelving reduce real-world capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most common mistake?

Buying a safe that is too small and only planning for current storage needs.

Should I focus on interior or exterior dimensions?

Both matter. Exterior determines placement. Interior determines usable storage capacity.

How do I measure for a closet?

Measure interior width, depth, height, doorway width, door swing, and handle projection.

Do thicker walls reduce space?

Yes. Steel thickness, fireboard, and door construction reduce usable interior space.
